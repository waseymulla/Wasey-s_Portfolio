Most Recent Projects and Research

Web Development
Overview:
During the summer and fall semesters of 2023, the Graduate Advising Office at the University of Texas at Dallas (UTD) faced challenges in responding to and maintaining the needs of current and incoming Computer Science (CS) students due to limited advisor staff. To address this issue, our team embarked on creating an automated solution to expedite the degree planning and auditing process for sophomore and senior students.
Approach:
We designed a user-friendly web application featuring three forms for inputting student information. The first form collects general student details, including name, student ID, major degree track, and anticipated graduation. The second form focuses on course completion, categorizing completed courses into different levels and categories. We implemented drag-and-drop functionality for degree track courses and manual entry for elective courses. The third form captures detailed information about each individual course, essential for GPA calculations and report generation.
Additionally, we developed an administration page to facilitate easyupdates to course offerings, degree tracks, and GPA requirements. This page allows administrators to modify existing data and add or remove degree tracks and courses as needed.
Technologies Used:
Our solution leverages JavaScript, PHP, SQL, and jsPDF for PDF generation. These technologies enable seamless interaction between the front-end and back-end components, enhancing the speed and accuracy of degree evaluations.
Results:
By implementing our automated tool, we successfully improved the advisor's response rate to students by 20%. This significant enhancement in efficiency has contributed to better student satisfaction and smoother academic planning processes within the Graduate Advising Office at UTD.

Database
Project Overview:
The research project aimed to address the challenges associated with query optimization in databases, particularly focusing on maintaining semantic equivalence while enhancing query performance. Our team explored the application of U-Semiring Algebra for SQL query equivalence verification, contributing to the advancement of database query optimization methodologies.
Research Issue:
Efficient query optimization plays a crucial role in improving database performance by minimizing the number of accessed rows. However, ensuring that optimized queries maintain semantic equivalence with their original counterparts poses a significant challenge. Our project aimed to address this issue by rigorously testing the equivalence of optimized and unoptimized queries.
Contributions:
Our project successfully validated the semantic equivalence of SQL queries through meticulous analysis and testing. We implemented the U-Semiring algorithm to systematically manipulate unoptimized queries and observe their transformations into optimized versions. This validation process involved exhaustive testing to ensure that the optimized queries consistently produced equivalent results to their original counterparts. Additionally, our project developed a Query Analyzer tool to facilitate comprehensive verifications, highlighting our dedication to ensuring both semantic and logical equivalence between query versions.
Technical, Algorithm, Analysis, and Implementation:
Our implementation replicated the research paper's methodology on a common test database, demonstrating the effectiveness of U-Semiring Algebra in creating equivalent queries. We imported a complex DBMS, utilized the sqlglot library for SQL manipulation, and compared the execution times of optimized and unoptimized queries. The project employed various strategies, including tree manipulation and query timing, to assess equivalence and optimize queries effectively.
Evaluation:
Our evaluation process included SQL query comparison and manipulation, showcasing an average 8% improvement in query execution times. Through rigorous testing and manipulation steps, we demonstrated the effectiveness of U-Semiring Algebra in maintaining semantic equivalence while enhancing query performance.
Conclusion:
Our research addressed the critical issue of query optimization and semantic equivalence verification in databases. By implementing the U-Semiring algorithm and developing a comprehensive testing framework, we provided valuable insights into SQL query optimization methodologies. The project's success highlights the potential of U-Semiring Algebra in advancing database query optimization practices.

Machine Learning
Purpose:
The purpose of the project "Precision Redefined: CNN and Bounding Boxes in Mathematical Equation Solving" is to advance the field of Optical Character Recognition (OCR) by integrating Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) with bounding box methodologies for the accurate recognition and interpretation of mathematical symbols, variables, and operators. The project aims to bridge the gap between physical and digital mathematics by developing a refined framework tailored specifically for recognizing mathematical notation. By leveraging cutting-edge technologies, the project seeks to facilitate the seamless transition from traditional pen-and-paper practices to enhanced digital workflows in education, research, and industry. Through detailed methodologies, case studies, and experimental results, the project aims to demonstrate the potential of OCR to digitize handwritten mathematical content effectively.
Languages and Tools:
Python: The project primarily utilizes the Python programming language for implementing OCR algorithms, CNN models, and data preprocessing techniques. Pillow Library: Python's Pillow library is employed for image preprocessing tasks, such as grayscale conversion and contour detection.
SymPy Library: SymPy is used for mathematical computation and equation solving, enabling the system to process and interpret mathematical expressions.
TensorFlow/Keras: TensorFlow and Keras frameworks are utilized for building and training CNN models, leveraging their comprehensive tools for deep learning tasks.
Matplotlib and Seaborn: These visualization libraries are utilized for generating graphs and visual representations of training/validation metrics and results analysis.
Effects of the Project:
Advancement in OCR Technology: The integration of CNNs with bounding box methodologies represents a paradigm shift in OCR technology, significantly enhancing the accuracy and resilience of mathematical equation solving.
Facilitation of Digitization:
By accurately recognizing and interpreting handwritten mathematical content, the project facilitates the digitization of mathematical equations, enabling seamless integration into digital workflows in education, research, and industry.
Improved Educational Tools:
The development of advanced OCR systems tailored for mathematical notation enhances educational tools and platforms, providing students and educators with efficient methods for digitizing and processing mathematical content.
Practical Applications:
The project's outcomes have broad implications for various practical applications, including automated grading systems, document digitization, and mathematical problem-solving tools.
